What are my rights as a citizen?

    As a citizen of India, you have several fundamental rights guaranteed by the Indian Constitution.


These rights include:

Right to Equality: This ensures equal treatment before the law and prohibits discrimination based on religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.

Right to Freedom: This includes freedom of speech and expression, freedom of assembly, freedom to form associations or unions, freedom of movement throughout the territory of India, freedom to reside and settle in any part of India, and freedom to practice any profession, occupation, trade, or business.

Right Against Exploitation: This prohibits trafficking, forced labor, and child labor.

Right to Freedom of Religion: You have the right to freely profess, practice, and propagate religion, subject to public order, morality, and health.

Cultural and Educational Rights: This ensures the protection of interests of minorities, including their right to establish and administer educational institutions of their choice.

Right to Constitutional Remedies: This enables citizens to move the court if they believe their fundamental rights have been violated. The courts can issue writs like habeas corpus, mandamus, prohibition, certiorari, and quo warranto to enforce these rights.

Additionally, India also provides certain other rights to its citizens, including the right to education, right to information, and right to work. These rights are aimed at ensuring the welfare and well-being of all citizens.
